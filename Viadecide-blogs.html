The Problem

Why product research is broken

The internet is flooded with reviews that exist to rank high and convert clicks — not to actually help you decide. Here's what's working against you:

💸

Pay-to-rank — Sponsored placements masquerade as organic recommendations on nearly every major review site.

📊

Opaque scoring — "Editor's Pick" badges appear with zero methodology behind them. How was the winner chosen?

📅

Outdated data — Articles published in 2021 rank on page one today. Prices, specs, and availability have all changed.

🎭

Hidden conflicts — Affiliate commissions influence rankings, and most sites never tell you this.

The Insight

Decisions need a system, not a list

The best decisions aren't made by reading more articles. They're made by applying a consistent framework — weighting criteria that matter to you, not to the reviewer.

Think of it this way: a ₹45,000 laptop is a very different purchase if you're editing video vs. attending Zoom calls. The specs that matter are completely different. Yet every top-10 list treats every buyer the same.

"The question isn't which laptop is best. It's which laptop is best for you — and that requires a decision engine, not a listicle."

Your Action Plan

Before your next purchase

Run through this checklist before spending money. Five minutes of structured thinking beats two hours of tab-switching.

Write down your top 3 requirements — not features, but outcomes.
Set a hard budget ceiling and a realistic budget. Treat them as separate.
Check the price fairness score — are you paying above or below market?
Run the Red Flag Detector on your shortlist before committing.
Verify the review date on any guide you use. Outdated data = bad decisions.
Look for affiliate disclosures. If there aren't any, be sceptical of rankings.
Check community signals — what are actual buyers saying 6 months post-purchase?
